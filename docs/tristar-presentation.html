The Problem

Loyalty is reactive.
It should be proactive.

Triangle members earn points, then manually decide when — and if — to redeem them. Marketers design offers in isolation, with no signal about when a member is actually ready to act. The result: millions of unredeemed points, low engagement, and missed revenue moments at the exact time customers are in-market.


A member who just bought coffee at Tim Hortons near a Canadian Tire is 4× more likely to shop there in the next 20 minutes. Today, Triangle has no mechanism to act on that signal.

The Solution

Three layers. One proactive engine.

TriStar is built in three composable layers — each independently valuable, exponentially more powerful together.

Layer 1
🎨

Designer

Marketer Copilot

A marketer enters a plain-English business objective. Claude Sonnet generates a complete, validated OfferBrief — segment definition, offer construct, channels, KPIs — with integrated fraud detection to block over-discounting and offer stacking before anything goes live.

  • Business objective → OfferBrief in <5s
  • Live deal scraper + inventory signals
  • Fraud check blocks critical risk offers
  • 3× retry with exponential backoff
Layer 2
🏛️

The Hub

Shared Context State

The single source of truth for offer lifecycle. Strict state transitions enforced — marketer offers flow draft→approved→active→expired. Purchase and partner-triggered offers enter active directly with a 24-hour TTL. Full append-only audit trail.

  • Immutable audit trail for every state change
  • In-memory (dev) / Redis (prod) store
  • Auto-expiry sweep every 5 minutes
  • Public read endpoints — no auth overhead
Layer 3
🎯

Scout

Real-Time Activation Engine

Scout receives purchase events, enriches context with GPS proximity, live weather, time-of-day, and behavioural history, then asks Claude AI: "Is this the right moment to activate this offer for this member?" If confidence > 60, the notification fires instantly.

  • Claude AI scoring with 3s timeout + fallback
  • CASL-compliant delivery constraints
  • Partner cross-sell (Tim Hortons → CTC)
  • 75/25 Triangle Rewards payment split

How It Works

From objective to activation
in under 30 seconds.

1

Marketer defines an objective

A marketer types a plain-English goal — "Reactivate lapsed high-value members this winter" — into the Designer UI. No form fields, no segment dropdowns. Just an intention.

Designer UI React 19 + Next.js 15
2

Claude AI generates a structured OfferBrief

Claude Sonnet receives the objective and any live deal signals (weekly flyer, inventory), then returns a fully structured OfferBrief: segment definition, offer construct (e.g. "5× points on outdoor gear"), channel priorities, KPIs, and risk flags — all validated by Pydantic before leaving the API.

Claude Sonnet 4-6 FastAPI + Pydantic v2 Deal Scraper
3

Fraud detection runs automatically

Before the marketer sees the offer, the fraud engine checks for over-discounting (>30%), offer stacking conflicts, frequency abuse, and cannibalization with existing active offers. Critical severity blocks the offer outright. Lower severity shows a warning — the marketer decides.

FraudCheckService Stacking threshold: 5
4

Offer flows through the Hub lifecycle

Approved offers enter the Hub as active and become visible to Scout. The Hub enforces strict status transitions, an append-only audit log, and auto-expiry. Purchase-triggered and partner-triggered offers skip the draft stage and are live immediately with a 24-hour TTL.

Hub Store (in-memory / Redis) HubAuditService Auto-expiry sweep
5

Scout detects a member purchase event

When a Triangle member makes a purchase (or a partner like Tim Hortons sends a webhook), Scout enriches the context: nearby CTC stores within 50km, live weather from OpenWeatherMap, time-of-day, day-of-week (weekday / weekend / long weekend), and the member's behavioural profile.

PurchaseEventHandler CTCStoreFixtures OpenWeatherMap API MockMemberProfileStore
6

Claude AI scores the contextual fit

Up to 5 active offers are evaluated. For each, Claude receives the full context bundle and returns a fit score (0–100), a natural-language rationale, and ready-to-send notification copy. If Claude times out (>3s), the deterministic fallback (Location 40 + Time 30 + Weather 20 + Behaviour 10) takes over automatically. Results are cached by context hash for 5 minutes to reduce API cost.

ClaudeContextScoringService SHA256 context cache Deterministic fallback
7

Delivery constraints gate the notification

Before firing, Scout enforces four independent guards: CASL consent check, 1 notification per member per hour rate limit, 24-hour deduplication per offer+member, and quiet hours (10pm–8am). Blocked notifications are queued with an exact delivery_time rather than dropped.

DeliveryConstraintService CASL compliance Redis rate limiting
8

Personalised notification fires — logged to audit trail

When score > 60 and all constraints pass, the notification fires. Every outcome — activated, queued, rate-limited, or error — is written to the Scout audit trail with member_id, offer_id, score, rationale, scoring_method, and timestamp. GPS coordinates are never stored (CASL compliance).

NotificationService ScoutAuditService No GPS in logs (CON-002)

Context Scoring

Business Impact

Why this matters for Triangle.

TriStar doesn't just improve the loyalty program — it rethinks it as a real-time revenue engine that rewards members at the moments they're most likely to act.

📈

Higher Redemption Rate

Context-triggered offers reach members at peak intent — when they're physically near a store and have just engaged with the Triangle ecosystem. Contextual relevance drives 3–5× higher redemption vs. batch campaigns.

⚡

Marketer Velocity

Designer reduces offer creation from hours to under 30 seconds. Marketers focus on strategy — Claude handles segmentation, construct, channels, KPIs, and risk assessment automatically.

🤝

Partner Cross-Sell Revenue

Every Tim Hortons purchase becomes a CTC sales opportunity. The 75/25 payment split model ensures CTC captures real revenue while rewarding the member for loyalty — not just give points away.

🛡️

CASL-Compliant at Scale

Built-in consent checks, 1/hr rate limits, 24h deduplication, and quiet hours (10pm–8am) ensure TriStar never spams members. GPS coordinates are stripped from every audit record by design.

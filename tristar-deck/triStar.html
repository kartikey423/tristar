🎤 Presenter Notes — Slide 2: Problem & Solution Press P to toggle ✕ Close
73% stat: Cite Forrester / Accenture loyalty research — most offers are irrelevant because they don't account for context. This is the $bn inefficiency we're targeting.
Architecture — Designer: A marketer types "Reactivate lapsed Sport Chek members" — Claude API returns a fully structured OfferBrief with segment, construct, KPIs and risk flags in under 2 seconds.
Architecture — Hub: The OfferBrief is schema-validated by Pydantic before every state transition. Nothing enters the activation pipeline without passing fraud checks. Every change is audit-logged.
Architecture — Scout: Real-time engine scores four signals — GPS proximity (<2km), time of day, weather, and historical purchase behaviour. Only fires when combined score exceeds 60/100.
Rate limits: Hardcoded guardrails — 1 notification per member per hour, no duplicates within 24h, quiet hours 10pm–8am. These can't be overridden, even by marketers.
Impact: +25% reactivation is based on comparable AI personalisation pilots. 80% faster creation is direct: 3–5 hours → under 5 minutes. Sub-200ms activation is our SLA target.

🎤 Presenter Notes — Slide 3: Benefits & Roadmap Press P to toggle ✕ Close
Marketer benefit: Emphasise the time saving — what took a CRM manager 3–5 hours now takes under 5 minutes. The fraud check is also automatic, reducing campaign risk without extra process.
Member benefit: Notification fatigue is a real problem. Because Scout only fires when all four context signals align, members get fewer but far more relevant offers. That's what drives redemption rates up.
Business benefit: Every offer has a complete audit trail. The schema-gated lifecycle means compliance teams can trace any offer from creation to expiry. Azure horizontal scaling means no capacity planning.
Phase 1 (current): All core functionality is demo-ready — Designer generates briefs, Hub tracks state, Scout scores and activates. Fraud detection covers all four patterns defined in the spec.
Phase 2 — Intelligence: ML targeting means the scoring algorithm improves automatically from redemption data. A/B testing lets marketers validate offer variants before full rollout.
Closing line: "TriStar isn't just a hackathon prototype — it's a production-ready architecture. The three layers are independently deployable, the schema is the contract, and every component has test coverage above 80%."

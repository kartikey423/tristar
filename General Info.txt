Teams Registration
Each team must appoint a captain to register the team and communicate with organizers and experts.
Participants must be registered through the official Hackathon Registration Site before the hackathon begins.
Teams should be formed based on the following rules:

Teams must consist of 1-8 members.
Teams can participate online or offline.
Each team will contain CTC team members only.
Ideas must be practical and feasible to implement beyond the hackathon.
Participants are expected to significantly leverage AI coding tools (Copilot, Claude, etc.) during development. Clearly describe how GenAI / Agentic solutions will be used while submitting your idea.

Judging Criteria
Projects will be evaluated on the following criteria (as an example):

Technology and Application: Integration and viability of AI, ML and LLM technologies.

Landscape and Positioning: Market/CTC fit and competitive advantage.

Relevance: Market/CTC need, scale and use case clarity.

Resonance: Human-centric design and long-term solution viability.

Revolutionary Impact: Innovation and significant market advancement.

*Projects should be developed during the hackathon and presented in the form of a Proof of Concept solutions.


Pitch delivery should follow 5+5+1 Rule
The 5+5+1 rule is a practical framework for managing time during hackathon presentations, ensuring smooth transitions and efficient use of the schedule.

5 Minutes for Presentation: Each team has 5 minutes to pitch their project, focusing on key elements such as the problem, solution, implementation, and impact. This keeps presentations concise and engaging.
5 Minutes for Questions: After the pitch, judges or the audience have 5 minutes to ask questions, clarify details, and provide feedback. This allows teams to elaborate on their ideas and demonstrate their depth of understanding.
1 Minute for Transition: A 1-minute buffer is allocated for transitioning between teams, ensuring the next group is ready to present without delays.
